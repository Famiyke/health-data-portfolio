CIHI Quick StatsHospital Performance
Canadian Hospital Readmission Analysis
Cleaned and analysed 5-year 30-day readmission rates across 10 provinces and 8 diagnoses. Identified conditions and regions driving preventable returns with COVID impact assessment and cost-savings modelling.
PythonSQLTableau
Key Findings
Atlantic provinces have readmission rates 1 to 2 percentage points higher than the rest of Canada
COPD and Heart Failure together account for 39% of all preventable readmissions nationally
Readmission rates spiked by 2.2 percentage points in 2020 and returned to near pre-COVID levels by 2022
A 10% reduction in COPD, Heart Failure, and Sepsis readmissions would save an estimated $27.5M per year
Readmission Rate by Province
02
PHAC CCDSSChronic Disease
Canadian Chronic Disease Surveillance
Tracked prevalence of 5 chronic conditions across 10 provinces from 2010–2022. Built age-stratified trend analysis and a provincial burden score to direct prevention investment.
PythonSQLPower BI
Key Findings
Mood and anxiety disorders grew 49% between 2010 and 2022, the fastest of all five conditions tracked
Diabetes prevalence rose 36% over the period, with Atlantic provinces carrying the highest burden across all conditions
Hypertension is 14 times more prevalent in the 75 and older age group than in adults aged 20 to 44
Three provinces were flagged as priority targets for chronic disease prevention investment based on composite burden scores
Condition Prevalence Growth 2010–2022
03
CIHI NHEX 2025Health Finance
National Health Expenditure Analysis
Used real CIHI NHEX 2025 data to map Canada's $361B health budget. Per-capita analysis by province and category with drug cost deep dive, scenario modelling, and Excel workbook with 4 chart sheets.
PythonSQLExcel
Key Findings
Newfoundland spends $1,711 more per person on health than Quebec, the widest provincial spending gap in the dataset
Physician services spending grew 65% between 2015 and 2023, outpacing every other category including hospitals and drugs
GLP-1 medications such as Ozempic are growing at 14.5% per year, nearly five times faster than generic drugs
Hospitals continue to absorb 26.3% of Canada's total health budget despite decades of community care reform
Per-Capita Spending by Province 2023

Ontario Health CSVAccess & Wait Times
Ontario Surgical Wait Time Analysis
Ranked 15 hospitals and 7 procedures on Priority 3 target compliance. Quantified urban-rural access gap, identified worst procedure-region combinations, and tracked COVID backlog recovery. Live Tableau dashboard published.
PythonSQLTableau
Key Findings
Rural hospitals average 23 percentage points lower compliance than urban hospitals for the same surgical procedures
Only 80% of Priority 3 patients receive care within the provincial target time, falling short of the 90% standard
Compliance dropped 15 percentage points during the COVID-19 disruption in 2020 and has nearly recovered to pre-pandemic levels by 2023
11 of the 15 hospitals analysed fall below the 90% provincial compliance target
Urban vs Rural Compliance
05
StatCan CCHS PUMFMental Health
Provincial Mental Health Outcomes
Analysed respondent-level CCHS microdata across province, age, income, and Indigenous identity. Identified highest-risk demographic intersections. 4-page Power BI dashboard built and published.
PythonSQLPower BI
Key Findings
New Brunswick (61%) and Newfoundland (62%) report the lowest rates of good mental health of any province
Respondents earning under $30,000 per year report poor mental health at 1.5 times the national rate
Indigenous Canadians report unmet mental health need at 4.6 times the rate of non-Indigenous respondents, the largest equity gap in the dataset
Anxiety disorders (12% prevalence) have now overtaken depression (9.4%) as the most common mental health condition
Good MH % by Income Group

Manitoba Health PDFHealth Equity
Manitoba Regional Health Equity Analysis
Extracted and standardised 9 health indicators from Manitoba Health PDF reports across 5 RHAs. Built composite equity score, correlated social determinants with mortality. Excel workbook with 4 chart sheets.
PythonSQLExcel
Key Findings
The Northern RHA records premature mortality at nearly twice the rate of the provincial average
Four in ten residents of Northern Manitoba have no regular doctor, the highest no-doctor rate in the province
Low income is the single strongest predictor of poor health outcomes, with a correlation of r = 0.94 against mortality across all five RHAs
The Northern RHA scores 0 out of 100 on the composite health equity index, the only RHA classified as Critical

04How Every Project Is Built
Step 01
Source Verification
Every dataset is sourced from a named Canadian government publication. Download URLs are in each README. Simulated data is anchored to published statistics and documented inline in the simulation scripts.
Step 02
Data Cleaning
Before any analysis, a dedicated script checks for missing values, out-of-range indicators, suppressed cells (CIHI uses "--" and "LV" codes), and coverage gaps across all provinces and years.
Step 03
SQL and Python Analysis
Core questions are answered in SQL for reproducibility and extended in Python for depth. Every finding is verifiable from the raw data. No black-box models — readable, explainable logic throughout.
Step 04
Dashboard and Brief
Tableau and Power BI dashboards built for non-technical health audiences. Every project includes a written brief a health director can read in 10 minutes and bring to a planning meeting.
